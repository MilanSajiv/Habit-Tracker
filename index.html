6-Month Tennis Progression Plan

You're just a couple weeks in - perfect timing to build proper fundamentals!

Months 1-2 (Now - Mar 2026) - Fundamentals

  • Focus: Grip, stance, basic strokes (forehand, backhand, serve)
  • Goals: Consistent rally of 10+ shots, basic serve in the box
  • Drills: Wall practice 2x week (15 mins), focus on form over power
  • Sunday Session: Play but focus on ONE technique each week

Months 3-4 (Apr - May 2026) - Consistency & Movement

  • Focus: Footwork, shot placement, volleys
  • Goals: Rally 20+ shots, 70% first serve in, basic net play
  • Drills: Cross-court rallies, approach shots, split-step practice
  • Sunday Session: Play points, not just rallying

Months 5-6 (Jun - Jul 2026) - Strategy & Spin

  • Focus: Topspin, slice, game tactics, match play
  • Goals: Play full sets, understand court positioning, add spin to shots
  • Drills: Topspin forehand/backhand, drop shots, overhead smashes
  • Sunday Session: Competitive matches with scoring

Weekly Practice Structure

Sunday Match Day (Your Main Session)

  • Warm-up (10 mins): Light jogging, arm circles, dynamic stretches
  • Rally Practice (15 mins): Forehand to forehand, backhand to backhand - focus on consistency
  • Technique Focus (20 mins): Pick ONE stroke to work on (see monthly goals)
  • Match Play (45-60 mins): Play sets/games with your partner
  • Cool Down (5 mins): Light stretching, especially shoulders and legs

Optional Mid-Week Practice (Tue or Thu, 15-20 mins)

If you have time after football/gym, hit a wall or practice serves:

  • Wall Rally: 100 forehands, 100 backhands against a wall (builds consistency)
  • Serve Practice: 20 serves focusing on ball toss and follow-through
  • Shadow Swings: Practice stroke mechanics without a ball (muscle memory)

Essential Skills for Your First 6 Months

1. The Forehand (Priority #1)

  • Grip: Eastern or semi-western grip
  • Stance: Feet shoulder-width, knees bent, ready position
  • Swing: Low to high motion, finish over opposite shoulder
  • Practice: 100 reps against wall, 3x per week

2. The Backhand

  • Two options: One-handed (elegant) or two-handed (more power/control for beginners)
  • Recommendation: Start with two-handed - easier to learn, more consistent
  • Key: Rotate shoulders, not just arms
  • Practice: 50 reps after forehand practice

3. The Serve (Hardest to Master)

  • Ball Toss: Consistent toss = consistent serve. Practice toss alone!
  • Motion: Throwing motion (like throwing a ball), not pushing
  • Contact Point: Hit the ball at highest point you can reach
  • Practice: 20 serves per session, focus on form not speed

4. Footwork (Often Overlooked!)

  • Split Step: Small hop as opponent hits - key to quick reactions
  • Ready Position: Always return to center of baseline after each shot
  • Side Shuffle: Don't cross your feet when moving laterally
  • Practice: Footwork ladder drills (YouTube has tons)

Quick Improvement Drills (15 mins each)

Drill 1: Crosscourt Consistency

Rally crosscourt (forehand to forehand or backhand to backhand) with your partner. Goal: 20 consecutive shots without error. Builds rhythm and control.

Drill 2: Serve & Return

One person serves 10 balls, other practices returns. Switch. Focus on getting ball in play, not winners. This is 80% of tennis!

Drill 3: Approach & Volley

Partner feeds short ball, you hit approach shot and move to net for volley. Teaches transition game and net play.

Drill 4: Target Practice

Place cones/targets in corners. Hit 10 forehands to each corner. Develops accuracy and shot placement.

Mental Game Tips for Beginners

  • Focus on Process, Not Results: "Did I use proper form?" not "Did I win the point?"
  • One Point at a Time: Don't dwell on errors, reset after each point
  • Consistency > Power: Getting ball back in play beats hitting winners at beginner level
  • Watch the Pros: YouTube ATP/WTA highlights - you'll learn positioning and strategy
  • Be Patient: Tennis takes time! Most people need 6-12 months to feel "competent"
